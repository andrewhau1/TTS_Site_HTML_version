1. Online Learning Resources:

  • Treehouse

    • https://teamtreehouse.com

    • Treehouse is an online learning resource that walks students through the basics to advanced material. Lessons are offered on many different languages and many more supplemental topics as well. Most lessons incorporate a video and a chance for you to interactively work on the topic as well. They are divided up into paths so you know exactly what lesson to move on to next.

    • There are several different pricing points:

      • Pro: $49/month

      • Basic: $25/month

      • TTS Special: $9/month for Basic. Click here to get the special rate:  https://teamtreehouse.com/signup_code/tech-talent-south

      • Char/Meck Library: Someone mentioned that you can join treehouse for free with your charlotte library card. I believe this is a limited option though and you may not have full access to all of the lessons.

  • Codeschool

    • https://www.codeschool.com/

    • Codeschool is very similar to Treehouse. It has a similar design of lessons being divided into courses and those lessons also have a video, followed by an interactive section. Codeschool also does not have as many options of courses to learn as treehouse.

    • Codeschool is $29/month.

  • Codecademy

    • http://www.codecademy.com/

    • Out of the big three online coding schools, I would probably recommend Codecademy first. Again this is very similar to the other other two. Codecademy however does not give any attention to any supplementals and focuses pretty strictly on the basics of a language. Also, no boring videos, strictly interactive coding.

    • Codecademy is FREE!

  • Lynda

    • http://www.lynda.com/

    • Another alternative is Lynda, and I have heard many many good things about it. The plus side to Lynda is it also offers a ton of courses on topics other than development (photography, design, business, marketing, etc.). Topics are also taught via videos, but from what I understand is they are better produced than the other sites.

    • Basic: $25/month with unlimited access to videos

    • Premium: $37.50/month with unlimited access to videos and downloadable project files.

  • The Odin Project

    • http://www.theodinproject.com/

    • This is a relatively new resource. In their own words, “This project is designed to fill in the gap for people who are trying to hack it on their own but still want a high quality education.” I like this site because it explains concepts in a different way than most other sites do (they use words like “smoosh” instead of “concatenate”). There are no videos or interactivity, but it is a great resource to read if you aren’t quite sure about a specific concept.

    • The Odin Project is FREE!

  • w3schools

    • http://www.w3schools.com/

    • If you have dabbled at all in web development then you have most definitely come across w3schools. This a great resource to learn all there is about html, css, javascript, jquery, sql, and php. I particularly find it helpful when I am looking to correct a specific problem. It’s a great site to just pop in and out of quickly.

    • w3schools is FREE!

  • Codequizzes

    • http://www.codequizzes.com/

    • This is a very basic website that is great for benchmark testing yourself as you learn how to code.

    • Codequizzes is FREE!

  • Coderbyte

    • http://coderbyte.com/

    • Coderbyte has a ton of challenges that can be completed in 1 of 5 languages. This is a great way to hone up your skills in ruby, as well as learn how to do the exact same things in other languages. Very good for rounding your self out!

    • Coderbyte is FREE!

  • Upcase

    • https://upcase.com/trails

    • Simple Tracks (or trails) for various different languages, frameworks and other web development themes (such as typography)

    • These trails are FREE but there are other options on Upcase that require payment

  • Ruby Koans

    • rubykoans.com

    • learn the ruby language, syntax, structure, and some common functions and libraries, as well as culture.

Games:

4. Online Text Editors/IDEs/Environments:

  • Cloud9

    • https://c9.io/

    • Cloud 9 is an entirely cloud based web development environment. It is perfect for coding on the go and can be used on any device and you won’t have to worry about anything! The cloud based server is always up to date, its a great looking site, and the text editor works very well.

    • There are a few hiccups though that can make it a little frustrating. However their support is really good.

      • Rails Server - https://docs.c9.io/running_a_rails_app.html

      • Github - http://support.cloud9ide.com/entries/21285626-How-do-I-push-my-Cloud9-project-to-GitHub

    • Oh and it’s completely FREE!

  • Nitrous.io

    • https://www.nitrous.io/

    • This is very similar to Cloud9. You can do everything on the web, or if you prefer, you can download the desktop version. This will allow you to work in a text editor locally but run everything in a cloud server. Best of both worlds basically!

    • Free for a very basic version

    • $8 - $40/month for more features.
