Introduction to Game Development

Do you love playing games? Do you dream of building your own but have never known where to start? If you've got a passion for gaming and the desire to build your own worlds, characters, and adventures...this class is for you!

In our "Intro to Game Development" class, you'll not only learn all the key fundamentals of game development (developing custom behaviors and animation, scenery and character design, utilizing physics engines, etc.) but actually go from being a total beginner to being able to publish and share your own custom games! If you're ready to go from being a consumer to a creator of games, sign up for our "Intro to Game Development" class below!

Class Details:

This class is two weekends. Each day will be from 9:30am to 4:00pm with a 30 minute lunch break. The course is $850. This class is open enrollment (no interview is necessary). Whether you're a 16-year-old looking to write your first game, or a 60-year-old searching for a fun hobby, or wanting a peak into a great industry, this class is a great starting point.

About The Instructor:

Nicketas

Nicketas is a game design instructor, teaching summer courses at Emory and Vassar College (in NY), and a participant at numerous game jams. Additionally he is an author and professional life coach/mentor, helping clients accomplish their professional goals (whatever those may be!). He's been building game levels and mods since the late 90's, and has personally tested nearly every major game engine that has come about since that time. He has been using the Unreal engine and its various iterations since 2002, as well as having 4+ years building Prototypes in Unity. Together with his artisty, game design & coaching profession, he brings over 10+ years of combined experience.
